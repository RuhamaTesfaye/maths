# Lesson 2: Multiplying Regular Numbers

Now that you understand different types of numbers, let's learn how to multiply them!

## What Is Multiplication?

**Multiplication** is repeated addition. Instead of adding the same number multiple times, we can multiply.

**Example:**
- $3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12$
- Or: $3 \times 4 = 12$ (3 added 4 times)

### Multiplication Symbols
- $3 \times 4$ (times sign)
- $3 \cdot 4$ (dot)
- $3(4)$ or $(3)(4)$ (parentheses)
- All mean the same thing: "3 multiplied by 4"

## Multiplying Whole Numbers

### Properties of Multiplication

#### 1. Commutative Property
The order doesn't matter:
- $3 \times 4 = 4 \times 3 = 12$
- $a \times b = b \times a$

#### 2. Associative Property
Grouping doesn't matter:
- $(2 \times 3) \times 4 = 2 \times (3 \times 4) = 24$
- $(a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c)$

#### 3. Identity Property
Multiplying by 1 doesn't change the number:
- $7 \times 1 = 7$
- $a \times 1 = a$

#### 4. Zero Property
Multiplying by 0 gives 0:
- $5 \times 0 = 0$
- $a \times 0 = 0$

#### 5. Distributive Property
Multiply across addition/subtraction:
- $3 \times (4 + 5) = (3 \times 4) + (3 \times 5) = 12 + 15 = 27$
- $a \times (b + c) = (a \times b) + (a \times c)$

## Multiplying Large Numbers

### Method: Long Multiplication

**Example:** $23 \times 45$

```
      23
    × 45
    ----
     115  (23 × 5)
    920   (23 × 40)
    ----
   1035
```

**Step by step:**
1. Multiply 23 by 5 = 115
2. Multiply 23 by 40 = 920
3. Add them together = 1,035

## Multiplying Negative Numbers

### Rules for Signs

1. **Positive × Positive = Positive**
   - $3 \times 4 = 12$

2. **Negative × Negative = Positive**
   - $(-3) \times (-4) = 12$
   - Two negatives make a positive!

3. **Positive × Negative = Negative**
   - $3 \times (-4) = -12$

4. **Negative × Positive = Negative**
   - $(-3) \times 4 = -12$

**Memory Tip:** 
- Same signs → Positive result
- Different signs → Negative result

### Examples
- $(-5) \times (-6) = 30$ ✓
- $(-5) \times 6 = -30$ ✓
- $5 \times (-6) = -30$ ✓
- $5 \times 6 = 30$ ✓

## Multiplying Fractions

### Rule: Multiply Numerators, Multiply Denominators

$$\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d}$$

### Examples

**Example 1:** $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5}$

$$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} = \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 5} = \frac{8}{15}$$

**Example 2:** $\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{9}$

$$\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{9} = \frac{3 \times 2}{4 \times 9} = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6}$$ (simplified)

### Simplifying Before Multiplying (Cross-Cancellation)

**Example:** $\frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8}$

Instead of multiplying first, look for common factors:
- 4 and 8 share a factor of 4
- 3 and 9 share a factor of 3

$$\frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{4 \div 4}{9 \div 3} \times \frac{3 \div 3}{8 \div 4} = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{6}$$

### Multiplying Mixed Numbers

**Convert to improper fractions first!**

**Example:** $2\frac{1}{3} \times 1\frac{1}{2}$

1. Convert: $2\frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3}$ and $1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}$
2. Multiply: $\frac{7}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{21}{6} = \frac{7}{2} = 3\frac{1}{2}$

## Multiplying Decimals

### Method
1. Multiply as if they were whole numbers (ignore the decimal points)
2. Count total decimal places in both numbers
3. Place the decimal point in the answer

### Examples

**Example 1:** $2.5 \times 3.2$

```
  25 × 32 = 800
  
  2.5 has 1 decimal place
  3.2 has 1 decimal place
  Total: 2 decimal places
  
  Answer: 8.00 = 8.0
```

**Example 2:** $0.6 \times 0.7$

```
  6 × 7 = 42
  
  0.6 has 1 decimal place
  0.7 has 1 decimal place
  Total: 2 decimal places
  
  Answer: 0.42
```

**Example 3:** $12.5 \times 0.04$

```
  125 × 4 = 500
  
  12.5 has 1 decimal place
  0.04 has 2 decimal places
  Total: 3 decimal places
  
  Answer: 0.500 = 0.5
```

## Quick Tips & Tricks

### Multiplying by 10, 100, 1000
- **By 10:** Move decimal point 1 place right → $3.45 \times 10 = 34.5$
- **By 100:** Move decimal point 2 places right → $3.45 \times 100 = 345$
- **By 1000:** Move decimal point 3 places right → $3.45 \times 1000 = 3450$

### Multiplying by Powers of 2
- $\times 2$: Double the number
- $\times 4$: Double twice
- $\times 8$: Double three times

### Multiplying by 5
Multiply by 10, then divide by 2:
- $46 \times 5 = (46 \times 10) \div 2 = 460 \div 2 = 230$

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Mistake 1:** Forgetting to include all digits
- Wrong: $23 \times 45 = 115$ (forgot the 920)

❌ **Mistake 2:** Misplacing the decimal
- Wrong: $2.5 \times 3.2 = 80$ (should be 8.0)

❌ **Mistake 3:** Sign errors
- Wrong: $(-3) \times (-4) = -12$ (should be positive: 12)

## Key Takeaways

1. Multiplication is **repeated addition**
2. **Same signs** → positive result; **different signs** → negative result
3. For fractions: **multiply tops, multiply bottoms**
4. For decimals: **multiply, then count decimal places**
5. Use properties like distributive and commutative to simplify
